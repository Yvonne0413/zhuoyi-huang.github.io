Zhuoyi Huang

I am a researcher at xAI, working on reinforcement learning scaling and science. My interests broadly sit at the intersection of learning, reasoning, and embodied or multimodal intelligence.

Previously, I studied computer science at Stanford, and my research has spanned embodied AI, multimodal inference, long-horizon decision-making, healthcare AI, and representation learning.

Research

I’m interested in how learning systems acquire robust competence over long horizons: how they reason, act, plan, and generalize in complex environments, and how similar ideas can be translated to scientific and real-world domains. Across projects, I tend to care about evaluation, scalability, and building benchmarks or representations that make hard problems legible.
