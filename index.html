Zhuoyi (Zoey) Huang

I'm a Member of Technical Staff at xAI, where I work on scaling reinforcement learning for science and coding.

Previously, I worked at Microsoft Turing and Microsoft AI Development Acceleration Program, where I worked on RL training for long-horizon computer-use tasks and led collaborations on healthcare foundation models with Mass General Brigham.

At Stanford, I had the fortune to work with Jiajun Wu, Tobias Gerstenberg, Fei-Fei Li, Ehsan Adeli, Stephanie Chao, and Zhengyuan Zhou. I also interned with the Meta Creation ML team, the Microsoft Research Visual Computing Group with Jingdong Wang, and Tencent AI Lab.

My work spans RL stability, sample efficiency, self-play, multimodal understanding and inference, and long-horizon reasoning, as well as applications in embodied AI, media, and healthcare.

Outside of research and work, I co-founded Currents to build next-generation agent infrastructure for social intelligence.

Some critical problems I keep returning to are:
- Real-world RL: non-verifiable, sparse, delayed, and subjective rewards — how can agents co-evolve with humans in knowledge-rich and embodied worlds?
- Simulations and AI for science: how do we enable autonomous scientific discovery, and how do we build realistic RL simulations such as virtual cells, digital twins, and simulated societies?
- BCI and cognitive learning: instead of AI replacing human, how can AI augment human cognition?

I'm always happy to exchange ideas, explore collaboration, mentor junior researchers, and chat with peers and senior researchers. You can set up a 15-minute virtual coffee chat with me — on life choices, research, industry, or startup ideas — here.
