"Spiral of Silence" and the pitfall of AI search

What is social intelligence, and why might it matter more as AI search becomes the default interface to information?

This essay argues that modern AI search can unintentionally compress diversity of thought. When millions of people rely on the same answer engines, the result can be a narrower public discourse: the same synthesis, the same framing, and the same dominant viewpoint repeated back at scale.

The core concern is a digital version of the spiral of silence. People are less likely to voice minority views when they feel surrounded by a loud consensus. If AI systems increasingly surface averaged, majoritarian answers, they can amplify that effect rather than counteract it.

The piece makes the case for social intelligence as the missing layer. Search should not only retrieve the statistically likely answer. It should also help users understand disagreement, context, community norms, and where real human insight is concentrated.

In that view, the future is not just better retrieval or larger models. It is systems that map the social structure of knowledge: who is saying what, why it matters, and where the edges of consensus really are.
